## üéµ Understanding Hip-Hop Bass

### The Two Main Types

#### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **808 Bass**
**Frequency Range**: 30-60 Hz (sub-bass)  
**Characteristics**:
- Deep, booming, felt rather than heard
- Long sustain (1-4 bars common)
- Punchy attack with slow decay
- Often distorted or layered
- Locks with kick drum on downbeats

**Sound Profile**:
- Sine wave foundation (pure tone)
- Distortion/saturation on top
- Long ADSR envelope (Attack: instant, Decay: slow, Release: 500ms)
- Often with pitch glide/portamento between notes

**Production Tips**:
- Use 1-2 per bar (syncopation for flavor)
- Layer with kick drum for impact
- Add high-pass filtered harmonics for definition
- Use sidechain compression with kick (makes kick punch through)
- Compress heavily (4:1 ratio minimum) to control dynamics

**Example Pattern (Hip-Hop 120 BPM)**:
```
Beat 1 (downbeat): 808 on C1 (33 Hz) - 2 bar sustain
Beat 3 (syncopation): 808 on G1 (49 Hz) - 1 bar sustain  
Beat 4 (tension): 808 on C1 (33 Hz) - half bar sustain
Result: Deep, hypnotic, modern trap feel
```

#### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Sub-Bass**
**Frequency Range**: 20-60 Hz (ultra-low)  
**Characteristics**:
- Ultra-deep frequencies you feel in your chest
- Supportive role (under other instruments)
- Often moves with chord changes
- Adds weight to bass line

**Sound Profile**:
- Pure sine wave (no harmonics)
- Smooth, even movement
- Long, sustained notes (quarter notes to whole notes)
- Less attack, more sustain

**Production Tips**:
- One note per bar common
- Doubles kick on downbeats for power
- Use HPF at 80Hz for monitoring (sub won't come through studio speakers)
- Add sub to every kick + snare hit for punch
- Keep velocity even (no dynamics)

## üé∏ Typical Hip-Hop Bass Patterns

### Pattern 1: Trap Bass (Modern Hip-Hop)
```
Kick:  X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .
Snare:  . . X . . . X . . . X . . . X .
808:   X . . . . . . . X . . . . . . .
```
**Feel**: Syncopated, off-beat, spacious  
**BPM**: 140-160  
**Key**: Often drops on unexpected beats

### Pattern 2: Boom-Bap Bass (Classic Hip-Hop)
```
Kick:  X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X .
Snare:  . . X . . . X . . . X . . . X .
Bass:  X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .
```
**Feel**: Groove-oriented, syncopated with kick  
**BPM**: 90-110  
**Key**: Follows drum pocket closely

### Pattern 3: Chopped Bass (Chopped & Screwed)
```
Kick:  X . . . X . . X . . . X . . . .
Snare:  . . . X . . . . . . . X . . . X
Bass:  X . X . X . . . X . X . X . . .
```
**Feel**: Busy, complex, layered  
**BPM**: 70-90 (slowed)  
**Key**: Multiple notes per beat

## üéõÔ∏è Production Chain

### Recommended EQ (per frequency):
```
20-60 Hz:   Sub-bass only (808 underneath kick)
60-150 Hz:  Mid-bass body (808 main presence, bass guitar)
150-500 Hz: Bass tone (definition, clarity)
500-2k Hz:  Bass character (fullness, width)
2-5k Hz:    Presence (click, attack)
```

### Recommended Compression:
```
Ratio:        4:1 to 8:1 (heavy)
Attack:       5-10ms (fast)
Release:      50-200ms (medium)
Makeup Gain:  +6-12 dB (bass needs boost)
Sidechain:    Kick drum (duck 2-4dB)
```

### Recommended Saturation:
```
Type:         Tape or Soft Clipper
Amount:       5-20% (subtle distortion)
Tone:         Warm (add harmonics)
Purpose:      Add aggression and definition
Note:         Use AFTER compression
```

## üéπ In CodedSwitch

### When Generating Hip-Hop Bass:
1. **Use Complexity Level 7+** for syncopated patterns
2. **Request "808" explicitly** in prompts
3. **Specify beat sync**: "lock with kick on downbeats"
4. **Ask for distortion**: "add aggressive saturation"

### Example Prompts:
```
"Hip-hop 808 bass at 140 BPM. Deep sub frequencies 
(30-60Hz). Long sustain. Lock with kick on beat 1 
and syncopate on beat 3. Add subtle distortion."

"Trap bass pattern. Syncopated 808s on offbeats. 
Minimal hits. Spacious feel. 2-bar loops. 
Add sidechain effect description."

"Boom-bap bass line. Follows kick pocket. 
Syncopated with snare. 90 BPM. Classic groove feel. 
Mid-bass frequency focus (100-300Hz)."
```

### Pro Tips:
- **Layer basses**: Sub-bass (sine) + 808 (distorted) for full spectrum
- **Volume balance**: Sub should be -6dB from 808 (supportive)
- **Panning**: Keep mono below 100Hz (won't translate to systems)
- **Reverb**: Minimal on bass (use on drums instead)
- **Delay**: Only on percussive hits, not sustained notes

## üìä Historical Context

- **G-Funk** (1990s): Heavy 808s, extended sustains
- **Crunk** (2000s): Tight 808 syncopation, aggressive
- **Trap** (2010s): Sparse 808 placements, hi-hats on 16ths
- **Drill** (2020s): Minimal 808s, kick-focused, sparser

Each era has distinct 808 usage patterns you can emulate!
